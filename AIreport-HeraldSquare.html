Local service prompts skip the brand

Example query tested: “holistic acupuncture for pain relief in New York”
Today

Engines returned generic results without naming ACA Acupuncture & Wellness. The likely cause: same-name disambiguation (city names that exist in multiple states) and the absence of a dedicated landing page that ties holistic acupuncture for pain relief to New York, NY.

Why this is happening
  • City name "New York" is ambiguous — without explicit state context, AI defaults to the most-cited same-name city. Schema and content do not consistently disambiguate.
  • No landing page where H1, lead paragraph, schema, and meta all answer "holistic acupuncture for pain relief in New York, NY" together.
  • LocalBusiness schema missing geo coordinates and explicit areaServed entries that would force the right disambiguation.
  • Topical authority is thin: AI engines need cluster coverage (service × condition × location) to confidently recommend a source.
The fix sequence — across 12 months
DAYS 1–30
Create a dedicated "/holistic-acupuncture-for-pain-relief-new-york-ny" landing page
H1 reads "Holistic Acupuncture For Pain Relief in New York, NY". Lead paragraph (the SOP §0.3 target answer): names brand + service + full address + ZIP + service area + languages. Title tag and meta description mirror the H1. The dedicated page lets every signal point at one query instead of overloading the homepage.
DAYS 1–30
Ship LocalBusiness schema with explicit geographic disambiguation
Add MedicalBusiness / LegalService / Restaurant (per industry) JSON-LD with address.addressLocality="New York", address.addressRegion="NY", postalCode populated, geo.latitude / geo.longitude pinned to the storefront, and areaServed listing nearby cities.

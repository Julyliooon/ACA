Days 1–30 — Must-fix

10

Trust killers and compliance issues. Address before any other SEO work.

  1. 1.
    critical Required medical disclosure missing: med.disclaimer_present
    A medical disclaimer must appear in the footer or on a dedicated page (educational purposes / not a substitute for professional advice / consult a licensed practitioner).
  2. 2.
    critical Required medical disclosure missing: med.license_or_credentials
    Practitioner license number or board-certification claim should be visible on the homepage or About page.
  3. 3.
    critical AI search readiness: Not GEO-ready (44/100)
    Site is not positioned to be cited by AI search engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews). Customers increasingly start product / service research in these tools — being un-citable means lost demand. Run the BAAM AI Search Audit for live engine tests, competitor citation analysis, and a 30/60/90-day plan.
  4. 4.
    critical GEO sub-category: E-E-A-T / trust (0/10)
    Low trust signals — biggest reason AI declines to cite. Evidence: No Person schema detected (named author/practitioner).
  5. 5.
    critical GEO sub-category: AI-answerable content (0/10)
    AI Visibility runner did not produce data.
  6. 6.
    critical GEO sub-category: FAQ coverage (0/10)
    No FAQPage schema detected on any crawled page. Evidence: Add 5–10 FAQs per major page with FAQPage schema.
  7. 7.
    critical Different addresses appear on different pages of the site
    We found 12 distinct addresses across the pages we crawled. Customers, search engines, and citation directories all use a website's address as a trust signal — multiple addresses on the same site reduce confidence and can split local-pack ranking.
  8. 8.
    critical Website address does not match Google Business Profile
    The address on the website does not match the Google Business Profile address. Citation directories trust GBP first, so this mismatch can dilute local-pack ranking and split reviews/citations between two listings.
  9. 9.
    important Visible business name varies from the declared name
    The declared business name ("ACA Acupuncture & Wellness - Herald Square") does not match the name shown in page headings or og:site_name on 1 page(s). Search engines and customers use brand consistency as a trust signal.
  10. 10.
    important Multiple phone numbers appear across the site
    20 distinct phone numbers are visible on the site. Citation directories and Google use the website phone as the source of truth — multiple numbers split call-tracking, dilute review-link routing, and weaken local-pack ranking.

Days 31–90 — Technical foundation

13

Schema, hreflang, on-page metadata, NAP. Fix once and stop bleeding ranking signal.

  1. 1.
    important Required medical schema types are missing
    Per Google's E-E-A-T guidelines for YMYL sites, medical businesses should expose JSON-LD schema for: MedicalClinic, Physician, MedicalCondition. We did not find "MedicalClinic", "Physician", "MedicalCondition" on any crawled page.
  2. 2.
    important No site-wide medical disclaimer
    Add a footer disclaimer site-wide stating that the information is for educational purposes only and is not medical advice, and to consult a licensed provider before treatment. Required for E-E-A-T on YMYL medical sites and standard liability coverage.
  3. 3.
    important Fix: Map or Directions
    no Google Maps link or embed
  4. 4.
    important Fix: Review snippets
    no schema and no visible reviews on the homepage
  5. 5.
    important Fix: Image alt text
    17 image(s) missing alt text
  6. 6.
    important Fix: PageSpeed
    Lighthouse mobile run did not return a score
  7. 7.
    important Add Physician schema (required)
    Names the practitioner with credentials so Google can match queries like "Dr. Huang acupuncturist Middletown".
  8. 8.
    important Contradictory years-of-experience claims across pages
    The site contains different values for the same authority claim. We found values ranging from 15 to 20 for years-of-experience claims. Pick one canonical number and use it everywhere — search engines and customers both use round-number consistency as a credibility signal.
  9. 9.
    opportunity No editorial / fact-checking policy page
    Google's YMYL quality-rater guidelines explicitly look for an "editorial policy" or "fact-checking process" page. Adding one — even a short page describing review procedures and authorship — is a meaningful E-E-A-T signal for medical sites.
  10. 10.
    opportunity Add MedicalProcedure schema (recommended)
    One per service page (acupuncture, herbal medicine, cupping). Lets Google build service-specific knowledge panels.
  11. 11.
    opportunity Add MedicalCondition schema (recommended)
    On condition pages (back pain, insomnia). Pairs with MedicalProcedure to capture "acupuncture for X" intent.
  12. 12.
    opportunity Add FAQPage schema (recommended)
    Earns FAQ rich snippets in SERPs — adds vertical real-estate to your result.
  13. 13.
    opportunity Add AggregateRating schema (recommended)
